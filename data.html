Heart Disease Data Analysis Project

By: Kian Ranjbar

During my undergraduate studies at the University of Colorado Denver, I completed a data science course that provided hands-on experience with statistics, probability, Python, Microsoft Excel, and data visualization tools. A major component of the course was a long-term team project focused on analyzing heart disease data to uncover trends, correlations, and potential contributing factors.

Tools & Technologies

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)
  • Tableau
  • Microsoft Excel
  • Web scraping and data cleaning techniques
  • Statistical analysis and data visualization

Project Description

Working with three classmates, we sourced multiple public datasets related to heart disease by scraping and aggregating data from the web. After collecting the data, we cleaned, organized, and extracted relevant features for analysis.

Our study examined heart disease prevalence across a wide range of variables, including age, gender, body mass index (BMI), smoking habits, diet, physical activity, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, race, and geographic location within the United States.

Key Findings

  • Heart disease prevalence increased significantly with age, particularly among individuals aged 65 and older.
  • Males showed a higher prevalence of heart disease compared to females.
  • The highest number of cases occurred among overweight and obese individuals (BMI ≥ 25).
  • A strong correlation was observed between heart disease and high blood pressure and high cholesterol.
  • Unhealthy lifestyle factors—physical inactivity, poor diet, and smoking—were consistently associated with higher case counts.
  • Notable disparities existed across racial groups and U.S. states, suggesting the influence of socioeconomic factors and access to healthcare.

Data Visualization & Presentation

We created visualizations using Python, Excel, and Tableau, with all final figures consolidated into Tableau for the final presentation. These included age and gender distributions, BMI ranges, lifestyle factors, geographic heat maps, and racial comparisons.

Conclusion

This project strengthened my technical data analysis skills and reinforced the importance of clear data visualization and effective teamwork. It provided valuable experience working through real-world data challenges, from data acquisition and cleaning to analysis and presentation, and introduced tools and workflows that I continue to use today.
