Technology and Culture: My Journey in Writing

By: Kian Ranjbar

I never considered myself a strong writer—in fact, I thought I was quite terrible at it.

That perception changed with my final paper for an class English Composition course I took in college. I approached the assignment with low expectations, believing my work would be merely average.

To my surprise, my professor called my paper exceptional and said she would like to use it as an example for future semesters. This experience taught me that, sometimes, we underestimate our own abilities, and that hard work and thoughtful analysis can exceed our expectations.

About the Paper

The paper examines the societal, psychological, and cultural impact of pornography. It discusses arguments for and against regulation or abolition, exploring the complexities of consent, free expression, and social consequences. Writing it forced me to research thoroughly, think critically, and structure my ideas coherently—skills that have carried over into all my professional and academic work since.
